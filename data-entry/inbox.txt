About
Electricity is a unique type of Element in My Singing Monsters. It was introduced in the Version 1.1.6 update, released on May 9, 2013. It is primarily associated and manifested in Wubbox and its Rare and Epic variants, and since Version 1.4.0, the Wublins and their variants.

Every monster that has the Electricity Element is part of the Supernatural Class, meaning that all Electric-Element Monsters have some basic things in common:

They cannot breed nor be bred.
They need to "wake up" (be activated) before being able to sing or produce Currency.
The Wubboxes wake up after absorbing specific types of Monsters, while the Wublins wake up after absorbing Eggs.
In addition, unlike the Wubboxes, the Wublins can only live on Wublin Island, which has unique properties.

Notes
The Version 2.0.0 update came with a graphical facelift and changed the element icons. The appearance of the Electricity Element icon changed from Electricity Element (Old) to Electricity Element.
Much like how the Ethereal and Natural elements are connected to each other, Electricity shares this same kind of relationship with Fire, having a connection with it and working like its "Ethereal" equivalent.
This is further proven to be true on the Fire islands' active Wubbox bio, which says that "it's supremely satisfying to reunite the partnering Elements of Fire and Electricity in such stupendous fashion!"
Even though the element of Electricity is not present in Dawn of Fire, the Celestial of Electricity, Galvana, is present there on Starhenge.
Electricity is the only element in the Supernatural Class.
Celestial, Dipster, and Electricity are the only canon elements in My Singing Monsters that can't be obtained by breeding.
Every Monster with the element of Electricity is said to be at least partially mechanical.
When Wubbox was first released, the Electricity Element didn't have the smaller bits of electricity around its icon.
According to Big Blue Bubble, combining all five Natural Elements, along with the Fire Element, will instead result in the Electricity Element, making all Supernatural Monsters artificial hexes.
The Electricity Element is the only element to be associated with two classes, those being Supernatural and Superethereal

Monster Elements/Monster Sigils (or simply Elements/Sigil) are the distinguishing features manifested by almost all of the Monsters in My Singing Monsters. Most Monsters have one or more Elements that define which Islands they can live on, and the parents that are required to breed them. Elements are divided into classes, whose Elements share the same breeding behaviors.

Out of the 44 Elements currenly in game, 21 of them can be used to breed Monster hybrids with two or more Elements. The remaining are used to either represent a Monster Class or a Seasonal Event.

The order of the Elements (as visible in the Monster info menu, etc.) is based on each Element's internal representation, and thus, the Element List (and other instances that require multiple elements) will present Elements in that order.

Notes
Since so many elements are combined together to create Triple, Quad, and Quint-Element Monsters, some elements are more dominant/recessive than others - thus meaning that a Monster’s appearance might not necessarily represent every trait of its elemental configuration.[1]
The Core Seasonal Monsters were the first Monsters whose elements were introduced after the monsters themselves. Before June 25th, 2016, they used to have no element at all, though they were shown only in news promotions and were present in My Singing Monsters Official Guide. In Update 3.0.4, their elements appeared in-game.
Kayna is the only monster that has different breeding behavior depending on the MSM game that it is in. This makes it, in a way, the only monster with two possible Breeding Classes.
The Dipsters used to have no element, but they eventually received their element in June of 2017. Like the Seasonals, their element didn’t appear in-game until Update 3.0.4.
Some Magical Elements are extremely similar to symbols that already existed within the game, such as the Psychic and Bone elements, which are "present" in various places throughout the series, but mainly in the Wubbox Stockpile.
Gold Island has been represented through a “Gold Element” icon (a gold crown with four curled spikes and a green gem in the center) on the 1.4.0 version of the Map, and this “element” used to appear in the Memory Game. Both of these occurrences were removed with later updates, but the symbol still remains in use on the 4.3.0 version of the Map.
The Titansoul Element is currently the only one to have an uniquely shaped border.
Currently, Glowbes are the only monster species without a element.
