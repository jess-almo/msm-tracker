Epic Monsters
Epic monsters icon
“	Epics are the ultimate forms of your favorite Monsters. Rarer than Rares, they have unique Breeding Combinations per Island, generate even more Currency, and cannot breed. All of these traits come together to create the most impressive and coveted creatures in the Monster World.	”
— In-game description of Epics	
Epic Monsters are a class of Monsters even more elusive than Rare Monsters. They were added to the game on September 5th, 2018. Epics share the same song as their Common and Rare counterparts, with the exception of Epic Wubbox. Most Epics also share just about the same animations, but Epic Seasonals, Wublins and Mythicals have their own fully unique animations. Unlike Rares, Epics are incapable of breeding, and will not show up in the list of possible "parents" in a Breeding Structure. They also are not available to purchase in the StarShop.

Availability
For a complete list of when Epics have been available and which Epics are currently available, see Breeding Availability
Epic Monsters, similar to their Rare counterparts are only available for breeding and buying at select times. So far they have each been available for a week each after their official reveal, and have been re-released during other promotional events.

Breeding
For a list of breeding combinations, see: Epic Breeding Combinations
Obtaining Epics is quite different from Rare Monsters. Each Epic will have its own unique Breeding combination per Island. Rare Monsters are not required to breed or unlock an Epic Monster[1], so a player can for example, obtain an Epic Noggin before getting a Rare Noggin.

Breeding Patterns
Epic Monsters follow a set pattern for their breeding combination. Breeding patterns for different kinds of Monsters is listed below.

For Single-Element Naturals on Natural Islands, the combination consists of the Quad-Element, and a Double-Element that shares the element of the Monster. For example, Epic Potbelly's combo on Water Island is Shellbeat Air ElementPlant ElementEarth ElementWater Element and Dandidoo Air ElementPlant Element. Epic Potbelly and Dandidoo share the Plant Element.
On Fire Islands, Natural Singles have a combination of a Quad-Element and the Natural Double-Element that lacks the element of the Monster. For example, Epic Mammott's combo on Fire Oasis is Sneyser Air ElementWater ElementCold ElementFire Element and Quibble Air ElementWater Element. Epic Mammott and Quibble are both Natural Monsters that do not share any elements.
On Magical Islands, Natural Singles have a combination of a Quad-Element and the Fire Double-Element that lacks the element of the Monster. For example, Epic Mammott's combo on Light Island is Blow't Plant ElementCold ElementFire ElementLight Element and Flowah Plant ElementFire Element. Epic Mammott and Flowah do not share any elements.
Epic Kayna on Fire Islands follows a similar pattern to Single-Element Naturals on Natural islands. The combination consists of a Quad and a Double-Element with Fire.
Epic Kayna on Magical Islands has a combination of the Quad-Element with the Natural Double. For example, Epic Kayna's combo on Bone Island is Plinkajou Earth ElementWater ElementFire ElementBone Element and Fwog Earth ElementWater Element. Fwog is the only Natural double on Bone Island.
For all other Natural or Fire Monsters, the combination always will be a Triple-Element and a Double-Element. The Monsters will also always include all four elements of the Island, so the combo cannot be used on any other Islands. For example, Bowgart Plant ElementWater ElementCold Element and Maw Water ElementCold Element would not be a valid combination for an Epic, since it only consists of three unique elements. Epic Oaktopus's combo of Bowgart Plant ElementWater ElementCold Element and Shrubb Plant ElementEarth Element has all four elements on Plant Island.
Epic Natural Doubles on Magical Islands each have a Fire Triple-Element in their breeding combination. For example, Epic Furcorn's Light Island combo is Sooza Plant ElementCold ElementFire Element and Pluckbill Fire ElementLight Element. Sooza is the only Fire Triple-Element Monster on Light Island.
Epic Fire Triples on Magical Islands each have a Magical Triple-Element that appears second to last in Market order in their breeding combinations. For example, Epic Rootitoot's breeding combo is Rooba Plant ElementFire ElementPsychic Element and Oaktopus Plant ElementWater Element. Rooba lacks the Water Element, which is sorted second in elemental order of Psychic Island.
Epic Ethereals also follow this pattern on Natural Islands, and each of their breeding combos includes the Triple-Element that comes last when sorted by the order of Elements (Air, Plant, Earth, Water, Cold). That also means the Triple-Element lacks the element of its Island that comes first when sorted by the order of Elements. For example, Epic Ghazt's combo on Plant Island is T-Rox Earth ElementWater ElementCold Element and Furcorn Plant ElementCold Element, and T-Rox lacks the Plant element.
The Epic Quints on the Fire Islands follow this pattern as well. For example, Epic Candelavra's combo is Repatillo Plant ElementEarth ElementFire Element and Dandidoo Plant ElementAir Element. Repatillo lacks the Air Element.
However, Epic Fire Expansion Quads seem to follow a completely different pattern, though still using all four of their Island's elements. They are all bred with a Magical Quad + Single combo, Epic Fire Expansion Quads with the Air Element are bred with a Magical Quad + Kayna, Epic Fire Expansion Quads that don’t have the Air Element, but are on an island with the Plant Element are bred with a Magical Quad + Potbelly, and the others are bred with the Magical Island's respective Quad + Single. For example, Epic Yelmut, who has the Air Element, is bred with Blow't Plant ElementCold ElementFire ElementLight Element and Kayna Fire Element, Epic Edamimi, who doesn’t have the Air Element, but is on a Magical Island with the Plant Element, is bred with Gloptic Plant ElementWater ElementFire ElementPsychic Element and Potbelly Plant Element, and Epic PongPing, who also doesn’t have the Air Element and is not on a Magical Island with the Plant Element, is bred with Pladdie Earth ElementCold ElementFire ElementFaerie Element and Floot Fly Faerie Element.
On Natural Islands, Fire Islands, and Magical Islands, Epic Seasonals follow the pattern of a Quad-Element and a Double-Element. No Double-Elements have been repeated for two Seasonals, making it likely that the pattern is that a new Double has to be used.
On Ethereal Island, the combination for Single-Element Epic Ethereals always consists of two Double-Element Monsters that lack the Epic's element and also do not share any elements in common. For example, the combination for Epic Reebro Mech Element is Sox Plasma ElementCrystal Element and Kazilleon Shadow ElementPoison Element. Neither of these two Monsters have Reebro's Mech Element nor do they share any elements in common.
For Double-Element Epic Ethereals, there is no pattern, other than the fact that they will also use two Double-Element Monsters that do not share any elements in common.
On Seasonal Shanty, the breeding combination for Epic Core Seasonals consists of the two Core Seasonals that celebrate their Seasonal Event the farthest away from the Seasonal Event of the Epic. For example, the Combination for Epic Punkleton is Schmoochle and Blabbit. Following the timeline of Eggs-Travaganza, SummerSong, Spooktacle, Festival of Yay, and Season of Love; the two furthest away from Spooktacle are Eggs-Travaganza and Season of Love, which are the Seasonal Events for Blabbit and Schmoochle respectively.
On Seasonal Shanty, Epic Aux. Seasonals do not follow a discernible pattern. However, they all at least use two other Aux. Seasonals in their breeding combinations. However, no Aux. Seasonals have been used in more than two combinations.
On Shugabush Island, there is no pattern for Epic Monsters, since Shugabush must be present in every combination. However, each Epic does have a unique combination, with the exception of Epic Viveine and Epic Quibble.
On Natural and Fire Islands, Epic Mythicals follow the pattern of a Quad-Element and a Double-Element.
On Mythical Island, there is no pattern for Epic Mythicals, since Cataliszt must be present in every combination. However, each Epic does have a unique combination and one of its Likes being the corresponding Dreamythical of the target Mythical that would've been used for breeding.
Breeding Times
Besides Epic Seasonals and Epic Mythicals, all Epic Natural and Fire Monsters have a well-rounded breeding time of a unique odd numbered amount of hours per amount of elements. Additionally, Epic Single Element Monsters have consecutive odd numbered breeding times based on the order of their appearance.

Epic Ethereal Monsters, however have a well-rounded breeding time of a unique even numbered amount of hours. Plus, in Ethereal Island, all Epic Single-Element Ethereal Monsters' breeding times take only 36% of the time required to be bred or incubated than that of the Natural Islands.

Amber Island
Main article: Crucible
On Amber Island, Monsters cannot be bred. Instead, Epics are obtained by evolving a common or Rare Monster in the Crucible.

Wublin Island
Main article: Wublins#Epic Wublins
On Wublin Island, Monsters cannot be bred. Instead, Epics are obtained by unlocking a Rare Wublin's evolutionary potential with Keys, and then filling in the required Epics in its inventory.

Feeding Monsters
Main article: Feeding
Each monster needs to be fed four times before it reaches its next level. Increasing the monster's level increases the Coins earned per minute, as well as the maximum Coins earned. The Food for each level is the same for each Epic Monster as it is for their Common counterpart, e.g. Epic Noggin food per level is the same as the Common Noggin at the same level. However, this is not true with the Epic Seasonals or the Epic Wubbox, which require more food than their Common and Rare counterparts.

Version History
Epic Monsters were first teased on September 4th, 2018 on the My Singing Monsters account's Twitter and Facebook, with a post saying: "Noggin was scrolling through our feed and discovered something shocking! Read the first word from each of our tweets since August 20th to find out what has Noggin so rattled." When fans took the first words of each post, then scrambled the words around, they got: "Scaratar was just the beginning, anniversary month is about to get a little bit more EPIC."

From August 30th to September 3rd, 2018, five different videos, with jumbled titles and descriptions were uploaded the official YouTube channel, each one being pieces of one big recording. Each one featured a mysterious voice commentating about the Monster World.

Eventually, on September 4th, 2018 a video titled "A Recorded Interview With Dr. Wardin E. Spurrit" was posted on Youtube. The video is a cleaner version of all the previous videos combined. In it, the voice now known as Dr. Wardin E. Spurrit talks about some new, unspecified monsters. At the end of the video, a red eye with black eyelids opens up and looks at the viewer. This eye would later be identified as Epic Noggin.

Many of the first few Epic Monsters were first teased or announced through YouTube videos. Two from the official account and 6 from YouTubers who were granted the opportunity to reveal them. Epic Maw was different to the rest because it was first teased in an Extra Life livestream on November 3rd, 2018, but it was also later given it's own reveal from the official account

Mechanics
To breed, select two Monsters of different species that are at least level 4 in the Breeding Structure, in any order you'd like. A new Monster will result consisting of all Elements combined, unless an Element is repeated. If an Element is repeated, the resulting Monster will be one of its parents. For example, Drumpler (Earth Element & Cold Element) and Mammott (Cold Element) will not result in a new Monster because the Cold (Cold Element) Element is present in both Monsters. Correct combinations can also often lead to resulting in the parent Monsters, with the exception of Natural Double-Element Monsters.

Some special Monsters like Ethereals, Legendary Monsters, Mythicals, Seasonals, and Epics aren't determined by a specific Element combination, and instead need two specific Monsters.

Increasing Odds
When breeding Monsters, the game first determines a set of possible results based on the two parent Monsters and the Island they are on. Each possible result starts with its own hidden base chance. These base chances are not equal, common Monsters are weighted more heavily, while rarer Monsters start with much lower odds.

Monster levels then adjust these chances. Only levels above Level 4 matter, and higher levels increase the chance of rarer outcomes more than common ones. Each parent contributes to this bonus, and Monsters with more elements contribute slightly more. This means leveling both parents improves your odds, especially when aiming for harder-to-get Monsters, though it does not guarantee success.

Wishing Torches further modify the odds by shifting probability from common results to the rarest possible result for that breeding combination. The more torches that are lit, the larger this shift becomes. The torch effect can be strengthened by Island Skins, limited-time events, and certain bonuses. Importantly, torches do not add new outcomes or increase total luck, they simply move chance away from common Monsters and toward rare ones.

After the game selects a breeding result, it may perform an additional check to see if that result becomes a Rare variant (when available). This check is influenced by torches, events, and special cases such as breeding a Common Monster with its Rare form. Breeding structures, time reductions, and previous attempts do not affect the outcome; each breeding attempt is calculated independently.

Another technique is to use a combination that will take the least amount of time, so that you can attempt breeding again sooner. For example, Ghazt's breeding combo on Plant Island requires Entbrat plus a Triple-Element Monster. Entbrat + T-Rox can result in a T-Rox, which takes 8 hours, while Entbrat + Pummel, Bowgart, or Clamble all can result in a Triple-Elemental that takes 12 hours.

Breeding Combinations
Main article: Breeding Combinations
Double Element Monsters
Doubles have traditionally the highest chance to be bred out of everything that's breedable where they are at, and just require two Single-Element Monsters. Natural Doubles have a 100% success rate, and thus cannot result in one of the parents. However, the breeding of Double-Element Non-Natural Monster have a slightly lower chance to succeed and can result in one of the parent Monsters.

For example, Maw (Water Element + Cold Element) is bred by breeding Mammott (Cold Element) and Toe Jammer (Water Element).

Triple Element Monsters
Triples have a lower chance to be bred than Double-Elements, and require a Double-Element and single Element Monster. Breeding combinations for Triple-Element Monsters are not guaranteed, and thus can result in one of the parents instead.

For example, Congle (Water Element + Cold Element + Air Element) can be bred by breeding Tweedle (Air Element) and Maw (Water Element + Cold Element), and also Mammott (Cold Element) and Quibble (Water Element + Air Element), and Toe Jammer (Water Element) and Pango (Cold Element + Air Element).

Quad Element Monsters
Quads have a lower chance to be bred than Triple-Elements, and in the case of Fire and Magical Monsters, can take multiple attempts. Since Quad-Element Monsters have many Elements, many combinations exist. Breeding a Triple with a Single is much more successful than breeding two Doubles.

For example, Sneyser (Water Element + Cold Element + Air Element + Fire Element) is bred by breeding Congle (Water Element + Cold Element + Air Element) and Kayna (Fire Element), and every combination of Triple-Elements and Single-Elements whose combined elements account to Sneyser's. But also, Sneyser can also be bred by breeding Quibble (Water Element + Air Element) and Boskus (Cold Element + Fire Element), and thus every combination of two Double-Elements whose combined elements account to Sneyser's.

Rare Monsters
Main article: Rare Monsters
Rare Monsters are special variants of existing Monsters that have a lower chance to obtain and only available for limited times. They have the same combination as their Common counterpart, with the exception of Rare Singles, which are bred with two different Triple-Element Monsters that share the same Element as the Single. For example, Bowgart (Plant Element + Water Element + Cold Element) and Pummel (Plant Element + Earth Element + Water Element) can result in either Rare Potbelly or Rare Toe Jammer since they share the Plant and Water Elements.

Once you have the Common and Rare variants of a Monster, you can breed them together to have a 100% chance of getting that Monster's Common variant. Breeding a Triple-Element Monster with its Rare variant cannot result in a Rare Single-Element Monster.

Epic Monsters
Main article: Epic Monsters
Epic Monsters are special variants that have even lower chances to get than Rare Monsters, and they are also only available for limited times. Instead of sharing a breeding combination with their Rare and Common counterparts, each Epic Monster has a unique combination that differs from Island to Island, and do not have a clear combination mostly. Epic Monsters cannot be selected to breed in the Breeding Structure.

Seasonal Monsters
Main article: Seasonal Monsters
Seasonal Monsters are special Monsters available for limited times during their respective Seasonal Events, or on other occasions, such as an Out-of-Season event or during Anniversary Month. Each Seasonal has a unique combination that is used across all Islands (except Seasonal Shanty), and has a high likelihood of failure.

On Seasonal Shanty, Common Seasonals are always available to breed. They are divided into three types:

Core Seasonals, being Punkleton, Yool, Schmoochle, Blabbit, and Hoola
Aux. Seasonals, being Gobbleygourd, Clavavera, Viveine, Carillong, Whiz-bang, Monculus, Ffidyll, Boo'qwurm, and Spurrit
A class simply referred to as "Seasonals", with just Jam Boree
The Core Seasonals cannot be bred without already having the Monster, so they must be teleported or bought. Each non-Core Seasonal, has a specific combination of two Core Seasonals. Typically, the combination contains the two Core Seasonals that the Aux. Seasonal/Seasonal is closest to. For example, Ffidyll is bred with Schmoochle and Blabbit, as Cloverspell occurs between these Seasonal Events.

Ethereals
Ethereals are special Monsters that are always available to breed. Each Natural Island has a Single-Element Ethereal available. They can be bred with a Quad-Element and Triple-Element Monster. Ethereal combinations have a 1% chance of success, but occasionally Ethereal Breeding Bonanza weekend promotions will increase this to 5%.

On Ethereal Island, Single Element Ethereals cannot be bred without already having the Monster, so they must be teleported or bought. Double-Element Ethereals are bred just like any other Double.

Paironormals
Paironormals are special Monsters that are available to breed, but the times depend on the player's timezone. They can be bred with a Quad-Element and Triple-Element Monster.

On Paironormal Carnival, Single Element Paironormals cannot be bred without already having the Monster, so they must be transposed or bought. Higher Element Paironormals are bred just like any other higher Element Monster. Although breeding is only possible on the Major Mode, due to the Breeding Structure being permanently locked on the Minor Mode.

Shugafam
Shugabush is a special Monster only breedable on Plant Island. Like other special Monsters, it has a unique combination with a low rate of success. It can be bred with Bowgart and Clamble. Mythicals
Mythical Monsters are another special class of Monsters with a special combination with a low rate of success. Each Mythical has a breeding combination of two specific Triple-Elemental Monsters, depending on the Mythical. However, on Mythical Island, the Single-Element Mythicals gain the ability to breed with the pure Dream Element Mythical, Cataliszt, to create Dreamythicals. Similarly to how each member of the Shugafam on Shugabush Island is bred as a result of breeding Shugabush with a specific Natural Monster, each Dreamythical on Mythical Island is bred using Cataliszt and a specific Mythical Monster from the Natural and Fire Islands.

Non-Breedable Monsters
Some Monsters are not available to breed and must be purchased from the Market instead. These Monsters include: Werdos, Dipsters, and all variants of Wubbox. Celestials, Wublins, and Vessels from Amber Island must be purchased from the Market and awakened by zapping eggs into them. Variants on Amber Island also cannot be bred as they acquired from the Crucible/Enhanced Crucible.

Getting Duplicates
When you already have a Monster and want to get more of the same Monster, there are different breeding combinations with higher chances of success that can be used. These combinations can also be used to get Rare variants more reliably. The best combination to use is the Monster you want + its Rare counterpart (Common + Rare), which results in the Monster 100% of the time. If a Monster's Rare variant is not available to you, there are other combinations that can be used. Quad + Single will result in a single 100% of the time. The normal combination for Doubles (Single + Single) is also always guaranteed. For Triples, the next best combination is Quad + Triple, which can infrequently result in an Ethereal or Quad.

Natural Quads don't have a better combination without using a Rare other than Triple + Single. However Fire and Magical Quads have an increased chance by using the same combination for Epic Kayna. When it was added to the game, its combination increased the chances of getting a Quad to make it have a lower chance to breed. This is the case even when Epic Kayna is not available.

Special Monsters (not Natural, Fire, or Magical) often have no alternate combinations. However Ethereals and Seasonals can also be obtained 100% of the time when using the same Common + Rare variant on Ethereal Island and Seasonal Shanty.

Costumes
Main article: Costumes
Costumes are infrequently obtained by breeding. Instead of requiring a special combination, each Costume is obtained using any combo that would result in that Monster. Chances to get Costumes can only be increased through random bonuses such as Clubbox rewards or random collects from monsters linked to the Titansoul of the island. But, since these events happen randomly, there is no particular guaranteed strategy to trigger them.

Rare Monsters are an elusive class of Monsters that are unique variants of regular Monsters that are only available during limited-time promotions. Because they are hard to obtain, they are able to produce more Currency, and take longer to breed and hatch. They share many traits with their Common counterparts, including their song, animation, and breeding combination. However, Rare Wubbox has a unique song and animation, due to the extra difficulty to obtain it.

Since Rares were first discovered on October 10th, 2014, several Monster classes have been given Rares, including Natural, Fire, Ethereal, Seasonal, Magical, Mythical, and Supernatural Monsters. Monsters of the Celestial, Dipsters, Legendary, Spectral, Titansoul, Primordial, and Paironormal classes are the only types of Monsters to have no Rare versions. Celestials, however, are capable of ascending into Adult Celestials, which work similarly to Rares, and used to use the rare icon in the Book of Monsters.

Breeding

Rares can only be bred or bought if they are available during a special promotion. Almost all Rares have the same breeding combination as their Common counterparts, with the exception of Rare single-element Monsters. They can be bred with two triple-element Monsters that share the same element that the Rare single-element possesses. For example, Rare Mammott Cold Element can be bred with T-Rox Earth ElementWater ElementCold Element and Clamble Plant ElementEarth ElementCold Element, since both triple elements share Rare Mammott's element of Cold. This pattern also applies to Rare single-element Magicals, and Rare Kayna.

Generally, a Rare’s breeding time is the Common’s breeding time multiplied by 1.25, with an extra 30 minutes added onto it. Although there are some exceptions, with Rare Single Elementals being one example.

Breeding with Rares
It is important to know that using Rares in Breeding does not help improve breeding chances. So, Rare Entbrat and Rare T-Rox have the same chance to produce a Ghazt as their Common counterparts do. However, Rares do have a different benefit when breeding. When breeding a Rare and its Common counterpart together, you will get that Common guaranteed, which is especially useful when trying to get Monsters to Zap eggs. For example, you can breed Rare Riff and Riff together to get a guaranteed Riff.

StarShop
All Rare Natural and Fire Monsters can be bought during special events from the StarShop with Starpower, or bred during a special promotion. Rare Wubbox is always available for purchase from the StarShop on any of the Natural Islands, Fire Haven, the Mirror Islands, or Ethereal Island. Every two weeks, a Rare Trio occurs, which makes three specific Rares available in the StarShop. When a Rare is available in the StarShop, it doesn't necessarily mean that you are able to breed it, unless it is currently available in a different special promotion. The same applies vice-versa.

Amber Island
Main article: Crucible
On Amber Island, Monsters cannot be bred. Instead, Rares are obtained by evolving a common Monster in the Crucible.

Wublin Island
Main article: Wublins#Rare Wublins
On Wublin Island, Monsters cannot be bred. Instead, Rares are obtained by unlocking a Wublin's evolutionary potential with Keys, and then filling in the required Rares in its inventory.

Ethereal Workshop
Main article: Attunement Structure
On Ethereal Workshop, Monsters cannot be bred. Instead, Rares are obtained by evolving a Common Monster into a Rare by tuning up all of its Elements using the Rarefied Attunement Structure. The Elements that are available to be tuned up changes each week.

Version History
Rare Monsters were announced on October 6th, 2014 and made their debut four days later for the weekend of October 10th, 2014. After the arrival of the first Rare, new Rares released every consecutive weekend through the weekend of January 9th, 2015, except for the weekend of December 26th. After January 9th, 2015, new Rares came every other weekend beginning on January 16th. In between every second weekend, the community could vote on already released Rares to return in Rare Reruns. Later, on July 6th, 2015, the Rare Single Element Monsters were released and lasted for a whole week until another Rare Single Element Monster took over. They had unique breeding combos to other Rare Monsters and had different sets of Likes.

On November 16th, 2015, version 1.3.6 launched, making Natural Rares permanently available in the StarShop, alongside the addition of Rare Wubbox. This was later changed on September 1st, 2016, where Natural Rares were no longer always available and instead became available on a cycle. The cycles too the form of Rare Duet promotions, which were later replaced with Rare Solos and Rare Trios. These made Rare Monsters half off in the first 48 hours.

Rares of various other classes of Monsters have since released. Rarethereals first released on April 22nd, 2016, starting with Rare Ghazt; Rare Seasonals on July 16th, 2016 with Rare Hoola; Rare Fire Monsters on May 24th, 2019 with Rare Stogg; Rare Magicals on February 24th, 2021 with Rare Theremind; Rare Mythicals on October 12th, 2022 with Rare G'joob; and Rare Supernaturals (not Rare Wubbox; it already existed) on April 5th, 2023 with the first five Rare Wublins.

On July 20th, 2022, the Crucible was unlocked, making the Rare Fire Monsters come to Amber Island, starting with Rare Kayna and the Double Elements.

Rare Reruns were promotions that were held between 2015 and 2018. In these promotions, players were able to vote on Facebook between typically two Monsters and the winner would be re-released. These Monsters would then be able to be bred or bought from the Market.

In September 2015, another Rare Rerun Faves promotion was held, but only the top 5 winners were made available.

Notes
Rare Monsters, along with Epic Monsters, are the two Monster Subspecies.
Compared to their Common counterparts, Rare Monsters have different:
Likes. Their likes always include their Common counterparts. (Except for Rare Wubbox)
Coin rates and cost. Rare Monsters earn more coins and cost more than their common counterparts.
Breeding/hatching times. Rare Monsters take longer to breed/hatch than Common Monsters.
Egg designs. Prior to September 25th, 2019, eggs that contained Rare Monsters looked exactly the same as their common counterparts, except that a shimmer, or gleam, effect will appear across the egg from left to right in Breeding structures and Nurseries. This has since changed so that each egg has an entirely unique design along with keeping the special shimmers.
Before Rare Monsters had their own egg designs, they had the same designs as their common counterparts. The developers intentionally did this to reference The Ugly Duckling.
Their official reply was:, "Hi there! There is no error - the eggs of Rare Monsters are designed to be the same as their Common counterparts. It's sort of like how the Ugly Duckling's egg looked like the duck eggs... until it hatched. Players will have to pay special attention to breeding and incubation times so that they know when a Rare is on its way!" This was also before Rare Monsters had a shimmer around their eggs.
Compared to their Common counterparts, Rare Monsters have the same:
Bed space and size. Rare Monsters require the same bed space and have the same size as their counterparts except for the Rare Wubbox, which requires only one bed.
Sound/noise, except for the Rare Wubbox. This stays true even if their songs have been altered in the Recording Studio.
Ability to be Teleported (or not) to other islands (other than The Colossingum, where Rares are unavailable).
Rare Monsters will count the same as their Common counterparts for the purpose of other Monsters' likes; e.g., if a Monster likes a Furcorn, it will also like a Rare Furcorn.
Rares can be bred with one another, but no evidence suggests that this offers a higher chance of successfully breeding a Rare. This includes breeding failure. For example, a failed breeding attempt with a Rare Furcorn might produce a regular Furcorn, not a Rare Furcorn. Of course, a Rare Monster cannot be produced using any breeding method if the breeding attempt is made outside the time when that Rare Monster is not available.
Rares can be bred with their common counterparts. This can be useful in making multiple Rares of the same type. Obviously, to do this, one of those Rares must already be owned. For example, an Entbrat can be bred with a Rare Entbrat and produce another Entbrat. (It would be possible to breed a Rare Entbrat with this method as well, but only when the Rare Entbrat is being offered). With Rare Ethereal Monsters, this sort of breeding can only be done on Ethereal Island. For example, a Ghazt and a Rare Ghazt on Plant Island cannot breed to produce another Ghazt; they can, however, on Ethereal Island.
For Special Occasions, Rare Monsters were dressed up at the same time as their Common counterparts, but the Rare and Common Monsters were always dressed differently from each other.
Placing Rare Monsters on Gold Island will be credited toward the breeding Goals with a reward as if they were their common counterparts. For example, if a Thumpies has not yet been introduced to Gold Island, a Rare Thumpies will still work the same and activate the 2,000,000 Coins reward.
Rares can only be boxed into a Rare Wubbox.
Rare Monsters from the Plant, Cold, Air, Water, Earth Islands, and Mirror Islands that also live on Shugabush Island can be bought from the Shugabush market during Daily or weekend Deals, bred on their home island and teleported to Shugabush Island, or bred on Shugabush Island by breeding their Common counterpart with the Shugabush. This breeding may result in a Common Natural Monster, a Rare Natural Monster, a Shugabush, or the Shugafam member that results from this breeding pair.
Since the introduction of Rare Wubbox, which does not have a Natural Element, all Rare Monsters are grouped as "Rare" plus in brackets what their common counterparts would be categorized as. For example, a Rare Furcorn would be categorized as "Rare (Natural)", which a Rare Wubbox would be categorized as "Rare (Supernatural)".
Rares were not available in the Windows version of the game available through Big Fish Games. However, on the more recent version available through Steam, they are.
As of 3.2.0, Rare Seasonals are categorized as Seasonals in the Book of Monsters, instead of Rares.
Most Rares have a maximum earning capacity that is 1.25 times as many coins as their common counterpart.
As of the 3.3 update, the sell prices for most Rare Monsters are higher than their Common counterparts.
Rare Viveine is an exception to this, selling for 12Relics on Amber Island, the same as its Common counterpart.
2018 is the only year after the debut of Rare Monsters without any Rare Monsters releasing.

The old Breeding Time format before version 3.7
The old Breeding Time format before version 3.7.0

After version 3.7.0, all times in game changed format. For example a time of 1 day, 23 hours, 59 minutes, and 58 seconds used to be displayed as 1:23:59:58, and it is now displayed as 1d 23h 59m. Seconds are only included for times less than 1 day.
Many Monsters have the same times, but for most of them you can think logically to tell them apart. A time of 8 hours could be a Pango, but if you're on Plant Island then that's obviously not it. You can also follow the link to the Monster's article to see if the combination you actually used could result in that Monster.
Other Monsters can't be told apart at all, such as the Shugafam on Shugabush Island, or in events where a combination can result in two Monsters that share the same times. An example would be a Rare event where both Rare Noggin and Rare Mammott may result from any Triple-Element Monster sharing the Earth or Cold Element.
Rare Quint Element Monsters take the record for the longest incubation time at 3 days, 23 hours and 30 minutes.
Epic Pinghound takes the record for the longest Breeding time, as the Common and Rare Quint-Element Monsters can not be bred.
Since 3.8.4, the Colossal Conundra can include time reduction Boosts. These Boosts apply multiplicatively on top of other boosts. For example, an Enhanced Nursery with the Sweti Settlement Island Skin and a 25% reduction boost would result in a (0.75 - 0.1) * 0.75 time multiplier, or a 51.25% time reduction boost.
Single-Element Ethereal Monsters on The Colossingum have the same incubation time that they have on the Natural Islands.
